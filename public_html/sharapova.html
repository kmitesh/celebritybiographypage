Maria Sharapova

Maria Sharapova is born April 19, 1987) is a Russian professional tennis player who as of May 12, 2014 is ranked world No. 7 by the Women's Tennis Association (WTA) and is the top Russian player. A United States resident since 1994, Sharapova has won 31 WTA singles titles, including four Grand Slam singles titles. She has also won the year-end WTA Tour Championships in 2004. The WTA has ranked Sharapova world No. 1 in singles on five separate occasions, for a total of 21 weeks. She became the world No. 1 for the first time on August 22, 2005, and last held the ranking for the fifth time for four weeks from June 11, 2012 to July 8, 2012. She has been in nine Grand Slam finals with a record of 4–4 and is playing in French open final on June 7th against Halep.

 Early Life and Education

Maria Sharapova was born on 19 April 1987 in Nyagan, Khanty–Mansi Autonomous Okrug, Russia, then within the Soviet Union. Her parents, Yuri and Elena, are from Gomel, Belarus, also then within the Soviet Union. Concerned about the regional effects of the 1986 Chernobyl nuclear accident, they left their homeland shortly before Sharapova was born, moving within the Soviet Union from Gomel to Nyagan.
